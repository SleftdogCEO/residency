Clinical Moments That Shaped Me

Vigilance

The Patient Who Appeared Stable

On a teaching shift, I worked with a resident caring for a patient who presented after reportedly using PCP. I expected agitation. Instead, he was nearly comatose. Throughout the shift, I reassessed him repeatedly. Near the end, his mental status improved—only for him to suddenly vomit blood. The room shifted instantly.

What stayed with me was not the intensity of that moment, but the vigilance that preceded it. Emergency Medicine demands constant reassessment and anticipation of deterioration, even when a patient appears stable.

Clarity in Chaos

A Family in Their Worst Moment

I helped stabilize a pregnant woman who had been stabbed multiple times with a screwdriver. As I dressed her wounds, I reassured her three terrified children and explained why she did not need immediate imaging or antibiotics despite their very real fears.

Bringing clarity and calm to that kind of chaos reminded me exactly why I am committed to this work. Leaving a professional and reassuring presence in crisis is a responsibility I value deeply.

The Internal AI-Guy

During medical school, I founded Sleft. I went door-to-door selling credit-card machines in Philadelphia. Often I walked in with little information beyond my ability to assess the situation quickly. Success required reading the room, identifying the core problem, adjusting in real time, and following through with real, tangible value.

Every patient encounter in the Emergency Department similarly begins with limited information and demands thoughtful, decisive action.

What I Value in Emergency Medicine

I am drawn to both the intellectual and procedural demands of the field.

I value caring for the undifferentiated patient, refining broad differentials, and tolerating diagnostic uncertainty while protecting patient safety.

I am equally energized by the procedural breadth—from laceration repairs and joint reductions to airways and resuscitation.

The specialty demands humility, adaptability, and constant reassessment—qualities I continue to develop with every shift.
